Planning your Trip

We appreciate you coming out to us to celebrate our marriage! If New Haven is a small city with a lot of great things near by. If you plan on staying in the downtown area (near the reception location) there is a wide variety of restaurants and bars to choose from, a grocery store, shopping, convience stores, and stuff to do all within walking distance. Here's a few tips to help you plan your trip.


Flying

New York(LGA,JFK): While these airports tend to have cheaper flights New York is about a two hour drive from New Haven (depending on traffic).

Hartford(BDL): This airport is one hour north of New Haven.

Tweed New Haven(HVN): This is the smallest airport of the three. Every flight must take a layover in Philadelphia before connecting to New Haven. A short 10 minute drive from the New Haven Omni.

Lodging

The Omni has given us a block of 20 rooms at a rate of $209 a night (without parking). Each room sleeps two people.

Ground Transportation

Car Rental: While there will be plenty of different agencies at airports, New Haven has Alamo, National Car Rental, and Hertz.

Parking: For those driving or renting a car just a warning parking in New Haven isn't cheap (~$20-30/day) in parking garages. There are hotels you can book a bit outside the city to avoid this.

Shuttles: Shuttles to and from airports can be coordinated. In general if you're traveling with a large group this can save you some money especially considering the cost of parking.

Haze and Brad's Favorite Bites

Olives and Oil: Modern italian food, crudo bar, wood-fired pizza.

Barcelona: Spanish tapas, great for V/VG and meat eaters.

Claire's Corner Copia: A V/VG cafe serving fresh breakfast and lunch items. Great baked goods too!

Stowes Seafood: A seafood shack that has one of the best hot lobster rolls around! (OK, well it's Brad's fav ...)

Haze and Brad's Favorite Bars

Elm City Social: Try the rubber ducky!

Ordinary: Try the smokey cocktail.

The Beer Collective: tons of brews.

Barcelona: a great selection of Vino!

Attractions

Louis Lunch: An old school char grilled burger. Claims to be the first burger joint ever.

Yale's Campus: Beautiful buildings, libraries, a cool cemetery.

Yale Art Museum: Paintings from pretty much any known artist. Cool historical artifacts.

Sleeping Giant State Park: About a half hour drive, great trails and rocks to climb.

East Rock Look Out: Either walk up via trails or drive to the top to get a birds eye view of New Haven that extends to the ocean.
